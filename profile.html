Legacy

Founded by the name of Villazón and Associates in 1968, it was the first design studio based in Mexico City. Manuel Villazón Vazquez with co-founders Jesús Virchez and Sergio Kiapa always tried to involve design in any posible aspect creating a desing environmet that used all the areas from arquitecture, graphic, industrial and editorial design. The studio was conformed by the design team used to create the image of the XIX Olympic that took place in Mexico in 1968. Manuel Villazón Vazquez was the pioneer of design in the aztec land, being the founder of all the design schools in Mexico at the Universidad Ibéroamericana. Now known by the name Diseño Villazón, the studio has a very important legacy that goes from architecture to art and has been an international multiprized studio since it’s foundry.
